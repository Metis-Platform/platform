If the goal is for **Metis Platforms** to become a portfolio of niche SaaS products, I would focus on products that share common infrastructure:

* Authentication
* Organizations/tenants
* Document storage
* AI assistants
* Workflow engine
* Reporting
* GIS/maps
* Property records
* Integrations

That allows you to build a platform once and launch multiple products on top of it.

## 10. Acquisition Assistant AI

This is the one I would seriously consider.

### What it does

User drops in:

* Property address
* Parcel number
* County ID

The system automatically:

* Pulls ownership
* Pulls GIS
* Pulls flood data
* Pulls taxes
* Pulls demographics
* Pulls nearby sales
* Builds an investment report

Then AI provides:

> "This property appears suitable for long-term hold. Risks include floodplain exposure and limited utility access."

### Pricing

$99–499/month

### Why I like it

This is not another CRM.

This replaces hours of manual work.

Investors pay for time savings.

## If I were building Metis from scratch

I would not start with a giant all-in-one platform.

I would launch in this order:

1. Property Due Diligence Workspace
2. Acquisition Assistant AI
3. Tax Sale Operations Suite
4. Property Acquisition CRM
5. Land Intelligence Platform

Each product builds on the same backend services and data model. By the time you reach product #5, you effectively have the foundation of a full real estate operating system without spending years trying to build everything at once.

The highest-probability business is probably the **Acquisition Assistant AI + Due Diligence Workspace** combination because it solves a painful, repeatable task and can be built incrementally by AI coding tools without requiring a massive engineering team.
